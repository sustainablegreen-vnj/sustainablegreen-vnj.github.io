Did you know, back in the 1970s, America was almost obsessed with asbestos, asbestos can be found almost everywhere. Asbestos is a group of minerals that are resistant to heat and chemicals. It is also made out of long thin fibers. Asbestos was commonly used because of those properties, and the fact that it was strong made it extremely useful in creating building materials (like insulation) and automobile parts. However, asbestos is also a carcinogen. A carcinogen is a substance, that can cause cancer. Asbestos is also works very slowly, sometimes taking around 40 years or more after the initial exposure to it. Plastic is a word that originally meant “pliable and easily shaped.” It only recently became a name for a category of materials called polymers. Plastic is everywhere, in everything. With how convenient plastic can be and with how cheap it is, it's hard to assume many corporations won't not use plastic. Microplastics are small particles of plastics that are sourced from the degradation of much larger pieces of plastic. These plastic pieces are usually found almost everywhere, however it's more commonly found in the ocean. This is because most trash are dumped straight into the ocean. These dumping cause serious damage for the marine ecosystem, it gets ingested by the fishes, birds, and other marine life. These effects cause serious long term damages, if not addressed immediately. With microplastics being everywhere, in everything, it wouldn't be a surprise if we don't find it in ourselves. Microplastics, wether you want it or not can be found in every single part of the human body. A research by the University of Rhode Island College of Pharmacy has found that microplastics are found in a mice's brain. This is also the same in humans, microplastics can make up 0.48% to 0.5% of the brains weight. There are so many health problems regarding microplastics, and in some cases, can lead to cancer. This reveals a concerning pattern, humanity has a reliance on materials that was once deemed revolutionary, next deemed dangerous. Just as asbestos was once seen as a versatile material, plastic has also been integrated in daily lives. However, the long term dangers far outshine the short term benefits, and we need to replace plastic before it can get worse. By learning from the past, we can address this problem immediately, before it becomes a larger problem. So, how can we actually fix this problem? Well, we can:
  • Reduce plastic use
  • Raise awareness, like:
    • Promote biodegradable alternatives.
    • Educate friends, family, etc.
  • Support research
