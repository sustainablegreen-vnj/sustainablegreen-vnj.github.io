However, not all is bad. 2024 has the chance to be the peak for global carbon emissions. This means that there is a chance from 2025 onwards, carbon emissions will finally start to decrease.

While we are currently (2024) transitioning to electric vehicles, most of us are still using fossil-fuel powered combustion vehicles. These fossil-fuel combustion vehicles are producing greenhouse gasses such as nitrogen dioxide(NO₂), which aren't good for Earth. As we continue to rely on these fossil-fuel combustion vehicles, the gasses they produce, like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and nitrogen oxides (NOx), contribute significantly to climate change and air pollution. These gasses damage the ozone Earth's atmosphere, trapping heat in our atmosphere, thereby causing global warming. These gasses also harm human health by attacking our respiratory and cardiovascular systems.

However, it's still not over yet. There are still many ways you can reduce the harm of fossil fuels.

  • Reduce personal use vehicles Rather than using personal vehicles, try using busses, or even the MRT or LRT. Indonesia's infrastructure has been developing extremely fast, and as a side bonus it's extremely cheap. You can also use a bike or walk to get to places, and those options are healthy, too!
  • Choose cleaner alternatives Whenever possible, transition into electric or hybrid vehicles. While this can be an extremely expensive investment, however the tax for owning an electric vehicle (EV) are reduced significantly.
  • Offset your carbon footprint Plant trees, volunteer into a corporation that helps the environment, etc.
  • Raise awareness If none of those options stated above simply can't be feasible, or if you wanted to make a greater change, simply telling others, wether it be friends, family, neighbors, anyone can help. Remember, it's not about how much you can change at once, sometimes the only thing that can really matter are the consistent little things.
Change isn't something that happens overnight, so the slightest amount of change everyday will eventually add up.
